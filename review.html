Vincent

This is my second try-out. As expected it was fast paced. Teamwork is very important. Although we maanged to fulfill the basic requirements of the project, having it to work, there are still lots of improvements that could be done. For example, the design of the car, the fittings of the wheels and walls, the power management, mechanical side management and much more. I am satisfied to see the fruits of our labour. I hope that I am more prepared for the future opportunities given to me to explore and get new ideas. In this try-out, I am in charge of everything, assisting my team members when they need help. In the project, we come out with a basic structure of how we want out car to be like and then we prototype it with cardboard, getting the dimensions of the car. Shahul then proceed with Fusion 360 to do the drawing and the laser cutting, I showed him how to do some of the components. Once he finished the drawing, he send me the finished the drawing to ask if there is any errors. After that, we proceed with the laser cutting. Meanwhile, while Shahul is doing the Fusion 360, Cheng Ee was doing the html with Jia Nan's assistance,Rusyaidi was doing the coding for the ultra-sound and the servo motors. However, the code did not run accordingly for the servo motor portion. After lectures from the lecturers, we combine the ultra-sound coding and the lecture coding, and it works On the last day, Rusyaidi and I did the tuning while the rest help Jia Nan with his assembly of the car components. We found out that the code runs accordingly when the audrino is powered by my computer, but the code doesn't run accordingly when we use the 6v battery power supply(4 x 1.5v battery). We then found out that in order for the code to run properly, we needed minimum of 0.6A and 8v power. We then change the power source to a portable battery and the problem is resolved I still do not get it how the 6V batteries cannot work, and i am hoping that I will be able to find the answer in future. To conclude, I am looking forward to more projects like such in future.

Rusyaidi

The try-out was an interesting experience for me and my team. Most of us had not seen let alone talk to one another. However, we manage to bond with one another almost immediately. This helps as all of us were able to communicate to one another effectively. I do believe the ice breaker game we did which was to make the tallest christmas tree was a contributing factor to our success as a group. For the try-out, we made a vehicle that was able to move and avoid obstacles. Although it was not aesthetically pleasing, we did manage to make a working and practical vehicle. We could improve it by making it more appealing to the eye but we were out of time. Hopefully in the future, my group and I are able to solve this issue and make our projects more captivating while still being practical. On the first day before we began planning, we decided to assign ourselves roles. I took the role to be in-charge of the coding as I was familiar with arduino and had a strong knowledge on how to use it. I did not do any coding on this day however as I did not bring my laptop so I proceed to help with the design. Later that night, I wrote most of the code for the arduino. Unfortunately, on the following day, I discovered from Vincent that servo motors are different from regular motors and required “#include ”. Some parts of my code are now useless. After researching on servo motors and getting helped from the lecturers, Vincent and I manage to get our code and all the parts working properly. On the last day however after we assemble most of the vehicle parts together, we discovered that the 6V batteries (4 x 1.5V battery) was not able to run the vehicle properly. After several testing and attempts to resolve this issue, we came to a conclusion that our vehicle needed a minimum of 0.6A and 8V power. We decided to use a portable charger or a power bank instead of the batteries given and it finally worked. Thank God! I do hope that all our future projects will be as fun as this one. :)
